* NOTES

A reminder of rankings and how they work

How are ranking points calculated?
A players total ranking points is calculated based on:
Best 4 tournaments in the last 12 months.
PLUS
Best 4 tournaments in the 12 months before that.
We're only using 4 tournaments per year so that there's no bias towards players that just turn up to heaps of events.

How many points do you get?
Points are allocated to players based on their finishing position within a tournament.
By default, an entry-level tournament has points allocated as follows:
Winner: 120 pts
Runner-up: 90 pts
Top 4: 60 pts
Top 8: 40 pts
Top 16: 20 pts
Top 32: 10 pts
A double-elimination has additional brackets in-between for people on the losers side.

Points are multiplied by 1.5 if prize money is $15k+.
Points are multiplied by 2 if prize money is $20k+.
Points are multiplied by 2.5 for Australian Open Singles.

Australian Open Singles

How do tournaments get included?
A number of tournaments are included as ranking events within the rankings system. We've aimed to get good coverage across the country, to be inclusive of players everywhere, and also only include them of $10k or more prize-money is available in the pool.
I'm happy to take request on new tournaments to include at any time.

What's the point of rankings anyway?
Bragging rights!
The rankings serve no official purpose, so you don't get anything for it. It's been put together to stimulate interest, and the website serves as an archive of results on past tournaments. To date, over 500 players are listed, with detailed results for over 50 tournaments dating back to 2014.
